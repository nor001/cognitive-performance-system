# Recovery

Strategic protocols for cognitive and physical restoration to maintain sustained high performance.

## Recovery Fundamentals

### Why Recovery Matters
- Prevents cognitive fatigue accumulation
- Maintains neuroplasticity
- Sustains long-term performance
- Reduces stress response
- Prevents burnout

### Types of Recovery
1. **Immediate**: Minutes to hours post-activity
2. **Short-term**: Day-to-day management
3. **Medium-term**: Weekly cycles
4. **Long-term**: Periodic extended breaks

## Daily Recovery Protocols

### Micro-Breaks (Every 90-120 min)

#### During Work Blocks
- **Duration**: 5-10 minutes
- **Activities**: 
  - Walk (preferably outdoor)
  - Light stretching
  - Deep breathing
  - Eye rest (close or distance gaze)
- **Avoid**: Switching to another cognitively demanding task, social media

#### Purpose
- Restore attentional resources
- Reduce mental fatigue
- Prevent physical tension buildup

### Lunch Break (30-60 min)

#### Structure
- **Nutrition**: 15-20 min mindful eating
- **Movement**: 10-15 min walk
- **Rest**: 10-20 min quiet activity or NSDR

#### Purpose
- Mental reset between morning and afternoon
- Digestive support
- Energy restoration for afternoon work

### End-of-Day Shutdown (15-20 min)

#### Protocol
1. **Review**: What was accomplished (2 min)
2. **Capture**: Open loops and next steps (5 min)
3. **Close**: Physical ritual signaling work end (pack bag, close computer)
4. **Transition**: 10 min walk or activity shift

#### Purpose
- Cognitive closure (reduces rumination)
- Clear separation between work and personal time
- Primes next day's work

### Evening Wind-Down (60-90 min before sleep)

Covered in detail in [Sleep](./sleep.md)

**Key Points**:
- Reduce cognitive load
- Dim lights
- Avoid screens
- Engage in restorative activities

## Active Recovery Strategies

### Light Movement (Daily)

#### Walking
- **Duration**: 30-60 min total (can be split)
- **Intensity**: Easy pace, conversational
- **Timing**: Morning, lunch, evening, or distributed
- **Benefits**: Blood flow, stress reduction, cognitive reset

#### Yoga or Stretching
- **Duration**: 15-30 min
- **Intensity**: Gentle to moderate
- **Timing**: Evening or post-workout
- **Focus**: Breath awareness, muscle release
- **Benefits**: Parasympathetic activation, flexibility

#### Swimming
- **Duration**: 20-30 min
- **Intensity**: Easy pace
- **Timing**: Off-training days or evening
- **Benefits**: Full-body movement, low impact, meditative

### Active Recovery Days (1-2 per week)

#### After Intense Training
- Replace HIIT with low-intensity cardio
- 30-45 min at 60-70% max heart rate
- Walking, easy cycling, swimming

#### Structure
- No deep work blocks (or maximum 1)
- Lower cognitive load tasks
- More frequent breaks
- Earlier end to work day if possible

## Passive Recovery Strategies

### Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR)

#### Yoga Nidra
- **Duration**: 10-30 min
- **Timing**: Afternoon (1300-1500) or when fatigued
- **Method**: Guided body scan, often lying down
- **Benefits**: Restores dopamine, reduces stress, improves focus post-session

#### Meditation
- **Duration**: 10-20 min
- **Timing**: Morning or midday
- **Method**: 
  - Breath focus
  - Body scan
  - Open awareness
- **Benefits**: Attentional control, stress reduction, emotional regulation

### Sauna Protocol

- **Duration**: 15-20 min per session
- **Frequency**: 2-4 times per week
- **Structure**: 
  - 15-20 min heat exposure
  - 5-10 min cool-down
  - Repeat 2-3 cycles if desired

#### Benefits
- Heat shock proteins (cellular repair)
- Cardiovascular benefits
- Growth hormone release
- Relaxation and stress reduction

#### Safety

### Cold Exposure

#### Cold Shower
- **Temperature**: Cold tap water
- **Duration**: 1-3 min
- **Timing**: Morning (for alertness) or post-workout (for recovery)
- **Method**: 
  - Start warm, end cold, OR
  - Alternate warm/cold, OR
  - Full cold exposure

#### Ice Bath
- **Temperature**: 50-59째F (10-15째C)
- **Duration**: 10-15 min
- **Timing**: 4-6 hours post-resistance training (not immediately after)
- **Frequency**: 2-3 times per week

#### Benefits
- Alertness (morning)
- Reduces inflammation and soreness
- Enhances mood (cold-shock proteins)
- Metabolic effects

#### Safety
- Build tolerance gradually
- Avoid if cardiovascular issues
- Not immediately after strength training (may blunt adaptations)

### Massage and Bodywork

#### Self-Massage
- **Tools**: Foam roller, lacrosse ball, massage gun
- **Duration**: 10-15 min
- **Timing**: Evening or post-workout
- **Focus**: Tight or sore areas

#### Professional Massage
- **Frequency**: 1-2 times per month (if accessible)
- **Types**: Sports massage, deep tissue, Swedish
- **Benefits**: Muscle tension release, relaxation, recovery

## Cognitive Recovery Techniques

### Attention Restoration Theory

#### Nature Exposure
- **Duration**: 20+ min
- **Frequency**: Daily if possible
- **Activity**: Walking, sitting, observing
- **Environment**: Natural settings (parks, trails, water)
- **Benefits**: Restores directed attention, reduces mental fatigue

#### "Soft Fascination" Activities
- Watching clouds, water, fire
- Gardening
- Birdwatching
- Non-demanding engagement of attention

### Novelty and Play

#### Purpose
- Engages different neural circuits
- Reduces stress
- Enhances creativity
- Provides psychological recovery

#### Activities
- Hobbies unrelated to work
- Games (board games, sports)
- Creative pursuits (music, art, cooking)
- Social activities

#### Frequency
- 2-3 times per week
- 30-60 min per session

### Social Connection

#### Benefits
- Stress buffering
- Emotional support
- Cognitive stimulation (different from work)
- Longevity and well-being

#### Implementation
- Scheduled social time (meals, activities)
- Quality over quantity
- In-person preferred over digital
- Balance alone time and social time

## Heart Rate Variability (HRV) Monitoring

### What is HRV
- Variation in time between heartbeats
- Higher HRV = better recovery and readiness
- Lower HRV = accumulated stress or incomplete recovery

### How to Measure
- **Devices**: Chest strap, wrist wearable, finger sensor
- **Timing**: Upon waking, before getting out of bed
- **Duration**: 1-5 min reading
- **Consistency**: Same time, same position daily

### Interpretation
- Track baseline (7-day rolling average)
- Day-to-day changes less important than trends
- Significant drops (>1 standard deviation below baseline) = consider rest or reduce intensity

### Applications
- High HRV day: Proceed with planned training and work
- Low HRV day: Consider active recovery, reduce intensity, prioritize sleep
- Chronically low: Address sleep, nutrition, stress, training load

## Weekly Recovery Structure

### High-Load Week

**Monday-Friday**: 
- Work deep work blocks
- Scheduled training (HIIT or resistance)
- Maintain daily recovery protocols

**Saturday**: 
- Active recovery day
- Light movement only
- Lower cognitive load
- Extended leisure time

**Sunday**: 
- Rest day or very light activity
- Weekly planning and review
- Longer NSDR or meditation session
- Prepare for upcoming week

### De-Load Week (Every 4th Week)

- Reduce training volume/intensity by 40%
- Reduce deep work blocks to 2 per day (instead of 3)
- Increase sleep by 30-60 min per night
- More time for passive recovery
- Purpose: Accumulated fatigue dissipation

## Stress Management

### Acute Stress Response

#### Physiological Sigh
- **Method**: 
  - Double inhale through nose (inhale, brief pause, inhale again)
  - Extended exhale through mouth
- **Repetitions**: 1-3 cycles
- **Effect**: Rapid stress reduction (30-60 seconds)

#### Box Breathing
- Inhale: 4 seconds
- Hold: 4 seconds
- Exhale: 4 seconds
- Hold: 4 seconds
- Repeat: 5-10 cycles
- Effect: Autonomic balance

### Chronic Stress Management

#### Assess Total Stress Load
- Work demands
- Training load
- Sleep quality
- Life events
- Social obligations

#### Reduction Strategies
1. **Prioritization**: Eliminate or delegate non-essential tasks
2. **Boundaries**: Set work hours, protect personal time
3. **Scheduling**: Build in buffer time, avoid over-committing
4. **Support**: Seek help when needed (professional if severe)

### Stress Tracking

#### Weekly Stress Rating
- Rate overall stress: 1-10 scale
- Identify sources
- Track alongside performance and recovery metrics
- Adjust systems when stress chronically elevated (>7/10)

## Quarterly Extended Break

### Purpose
- Deep recovery from accumulated fatigue
- Perspective reset
- Prevent long-term burnout
- Creative renewal

### Structure
- **Duration**: 5-10 days
- **Frequency**: Every 3 months
- **Activities**: 
  - Minimal to no work
  - Travel or staycation
  - Novelty and exploration
  - Social connection
  - Sleep as needed

### Preparation
- Complete projects before break
- Delegate or postpone tasks
- Set out-of-office and boundaries
- No guilt about unplugging

### Return
- Ease back in (don't overload first day)
- Review and plan with fresh perspective
- Integrate any insights

## Recovery Monitoring

### Daily Check-In (2 min)

**Morning Assessment**:
- Sleep quality: 1-10
- Morning energy: 1-10
- Motivation: 1-10
- Muscle soreness: 1-10
- Mood: 1-10

**Decision**:
- 8-10 across metrics: Proceed with planned activities
- 5-7 on some metrics: Modify intensity or add recovery
- <5 on multiple: Prioritize recovery day

### Weekly Review (10 min)

**Assess**:
- Average daily metrics
- Training completion and quality
- Cognitive performance (deep work quality)
- Overall stress rating

**Adjust**:
- Next week's training if needed
- Deep work allocation
- Add recovery protocols if struggling

## Common Recovery Mistakes

### Mistake 1: No Strategic Recovery
- **Problem**: Only rest when forced by exhaustion or illness
- **Solution**: Schedule recovery proactively

### Mistake 2: "Active Recovery" Too Intense
- **Problem**: Active recovery becomes another hard training day
- **Solution**: Keep intensity truly low (60-70% max HR, conversational pace)

### Mistake 3: No Mental Recovery
- **Problem**: Physical rest but continued cognitive strain
- **Solution**: Include activities that don't require focus or problem-solving

### Mistake 4: Inconsistent Sleep
- **Problem**: Trying to "catch up" on weekends
- **Solution**: Consistent sleep schedule 7 days per week

### Mistake 5: Ignoring HRV or Body Signals
- **Problem**: Following plan regardless of recovery state
- **Solution**: Adjust based on objective (HRV) and subjective metrics

## Recovery Hierarchy (Priority Order)

1. **Sleep** (7-9 hours, consistent timing)
2. **Nutrition** (adequate protein, calories, hydration)
3. **Stress Management** (chronic stress mitigation)
4. **Active Recovery** (movement, light exercise)
5. **Passive Recovery** (NSDR, meditation, massage)
6. **Advanced Protocols** (sauna, cold exposure - optional)

## Key Principles

- **Proactive, not reactive**: Schedule recovery before you need it
- **Individualized**: What works varies by person and context
- **Flexible**: Adjust based on measured and perceived recovery state
- **Systemic**: Recovery affects all performance domains
- **Progressive**: Build recovery capacity over time
- **Non-negotiable**: Recovery is not laziness, it's training
