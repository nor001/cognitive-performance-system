# Sleep

Evidence-based protocols for optimizing sleep quality, architecture, and cognitive restoration.

## Sleep Fundamentals

- **Target**: 7-9 hours per night
- **Consistency**: ±30 min window for sleep and wake times
- **Optimal timing**: Sleep 2130-2300, wake 0600-0730 (adjust for individual chronotype)

## Sleep Environment

- **Temperature**: 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- **Darkness**: Complete darkness or <1 lux (blackout curtains or eye mask)
- **Noise**: <30 decibels (white noise machine or earplugs if needed)
- **Bed**: Medium-firm mattress, supportive pillows

## Pre-Sleep Routine (90 min before bed)

- **120 min before**: Dim lights to 50%, last meal complete
- **90 min before**: No screens (or use blue-light blockers), low-cognitive activities
- **60 min before**: Prepare environment (temperature, darkness)
- **30 min before**: In bedroom, read or quiet activity
- **Lights out**: Consistent time (±15 min). If not asleep in 20 min, get up and do quiet activity until sleepy

## Light Exposure

- **Morning** (first 30 min): 10,000 lux or sunlight for 10-30 min
- **Daytime**: Bright environment (500-1000 lux)
- **Evening** (after sunset): Dim to <50%, warm colors, minimize screens
- **Night**: Complete darkness. If needed, red light only (<5 lux)

## Temperature and Substances

### Temperature
- Evening: Hot bath/shower (104-109°F, 10-20 min) 1-2 hours before bed
- Morning: Cold shower to boost alertness

### Substances
- **Caffeine**: Cutoff 8-10 hours before bed (no caffeine after 1400-1500)
- **Alcohol**: Avoid 3-4 hours before bed (fragments sleep, reduces REM)
- **Melatonin**: 0.3-1mg, 30-60 min before bed (for circadian adjustment, not nightly)
- **Magnesium**: 200-400mg, 1-2 hours before bed (glycinate or threonate)

## Sleep Aids

### Breathing for Sleep
- **4-7-8 Breathing**: Inhale 4 sec, hold 7 sec, exhale 8 sec, repeat 4-8x
- **Resonance Breathing**: Inhale 5 sec, exhale 5 sec, 5-10 min

### Other Techniques
- Progressive muscle relaxation: Tense and release muscle groups, start at toes
- Cognitive shuffle: Visualize random non-threatening images (prevents rumination)
- NSDR: Yoga Nidra or body scan (10-30 min afternoon if needed)

## Napping

- **Short nap**: 10-20 min (alertness boost, no grogginess)
- **Long nap**: 90 min (full cycle, creativity boost)
- **Avoid**: 30-60 min naps (causes grogginess)
- **Timing**: Early afternoon (1300-1500), avoid after 1600

## Tracking and Troubleshooting

### Track Daily
- Sleep/wake times, time to fall asleep, awakenings, quality (1-10), morning alertness (1-10)
- Optional: Use wearable (Oura, Whoop, Apple Watch) for trends

### Common Issues
- **Can't fall asleep**: Brain dump worries, use breathing exercises, earlier wind-down, reduce evening cognitive load
- **Night awakenings**: Check temperature/noise/light, limit evening fluids, assess for sleep apnea if persistent
- **Early waking**: Better blackout, consistent temperature, review caffeine intake
- **Non-restorative**: Increase duration by 30 min, reduce alcohol, manage stress

### Consult Provider If
- Chronic issues >3 months despite optimization
- Loud snoring with breathing pauses
- Excessive daytime sleepiness despite adequate duration

## Key Principles

- Sleep is non-negotiable for cognitive performance
- Consistency matters more than perfection
- Protect sleep time in your schedule
- When in doubt, sleep more
